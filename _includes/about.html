About

We are a program dedicated to tackling climate change. Our goal is to foster power in numbers among those who are most influenced by climate change to inspire others through empathy and in-person conversation. One of the most effective ways of changing or enhancing opinion is through listening empathetically and engaging in discussion on the issue.

Mission

People often feel powerless when it comes to tackling climate change — especially us teens, who are affected most by it. This program’s mission is to try different ways we can influence decision-makers, adults who can vote or play a larger role in making climate related decisions. We want to gain an understanding of how people react to different messaging — and communicate what works. Do people need facts presented, or be in the right emotional state? Do they need to believe they can help or that this will affect their children and grandchildren?

Rational

Over the past 500 years, carbon dioxide has increased exponentially, with burning fossil fuels for heat and electricity as the leading contributor to climate change. Teens and young adults believe that climate change will significantly harm their generation — about 7 in 10 believe so, a higher proportion than adults over 30. According to a 2019 poll of 629 teens by the Washington Post, 1 in 7 teens have participated in a climate change school walkout, and teens rank climate change much higher than adults in a list of major important issues. A Nature article reports that a quarter of adults in a 2018 survey believed that the purpose of fighting for climate change is “to provide a better life for our children and grandchildren,” suggesting that adults will act in order to benefit their children’s futures and that youth activism as helped illuminate this. Based on these studies, we hope to inspire other teens and young adults to have conversations with the adults around them — parents, guardians, teachers, mentors — about climate change. Together among the 42 million teens in the US, if we all talk to 3 adults each, we nearly reach the entire voter turnout in the 2016 US Presidential Election,. That’s huge. This is huge.
